Paulius Dilkas

I am a PhD student jointly at the University of Edinburgh (Artificial Intelligence and its Applications Institute, School of Informatics) and Heriot-Watt University. My work is supervized by Vaishak Belle and funded by the Edinburgh Centre for Robotics via a scholarship from the EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training in Robotics and Autonomous Systems. I received my MSci in Computing Science degree from the University of Glasgow.

My current work is mostly centered around weighted model counting (WMC) and its role in probabilistic inference. I am interested in WMC algorithms, how inference problems are encoded into WMC instances, and understanding the behavior of algorithms using parameterized complexity and random instances. Previously, I have worked on graph algorithms, algorithm portfolios, formal modelling with bigraphs, and inverse reinforcement learning. More generally, I am interested in various algorithmic problems motivated by artificial intelligence.
